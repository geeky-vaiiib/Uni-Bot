# SIT Examination and Academic Rules

## Academic Calendar

### Semester System
- **Odd Semester**: July - December
- **Even Semester**: January - June
- Each semester: 16-18 weeks of instruction

### Typical Schedule
| Activity | Odd Semester | Even Semester |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Classes Begin | July 1st week | January 1st week |
| 1st Internal Test | August end | February end |
| 2nd Internal Test | October | April |
| Semester End Exams | November-December | May-June |
| Results | January | July |
| Vacation | December end | June-July |

---

## Evaluation System

### Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) - 50 Marks

#### Theory Subjects
| Component | Marks |
|-----------|-------|
| Internal Test 1 | 15 |
| Internal Test 2 | 15 |
| Assignments | 10 |
| Quiz/Seminars | 10 |
| **Total CIE** | **50** |

#### Laboratory Subjects
| Component | Marks |
|-----------|-------|
| Weekly Lab Work | 25 |
| Lab Test | 15 |
| Record | 10 |
| **Total CIE** | **50** |

### Semester End Examination (SEE) - 50 Marks
- Duration: 3 hours for theory
- 2-3 hours for practicals
- Minimum passing: 40% in SEE (20/50)

### Total Marks
- **Theory subjects**: CIE (50) + SEE (50) = 100
- **Lab subjects**: CIE (50) + SEE (50) = 100
- **Projects**: Based on department guidelines

## Grading System

### Grade Points
| Marks Range | Grade | Grade Point |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | S (Outstanding) | 10 |
| 80-89 | A (Excellent) | 9 |
| 70-79 | B (Very Good) | 8 |
| 60-69 | C (Good) | 7 |
| 50-59 | D (Average) | 6 |
| 45-49 | E (Pass) | 5 |
| 40-44 | P (Pass Class) | 4 |
| Below 40 | F (Fail) | 0 |

### SGPA Calculation
SGPA = Σ(Credit × Grade Point) / Σ Credits

### CGPA Calculation
CGPA = Σ(SGPA × Semester Credits) / Σ Total Credits

## Attendance Rules

### Minimum Attendance Requirement
- **85%** attendance required in each course
- Attendance calculated separately for theory and practicals

### Condonation of Attendance
| Attendance | Eligibility |
|------------|-------------|
| ≥ 85% | Eligible for SEE |
| 75% - 84% | Condonation possible by Principal |
| < 75% | NOT eligible for condonation |

### Valid Reasons for Condonation
1. Hospitalization (with medical certificate)
2. Participation in university events
3. NCC/NSS camps
4. Sports tournaments (university level)
5. Technical paper presentations

### Consequences of Short Attendance
- Warning letters to parents
- Repeat the course next semester
- May affect internal marks

## Examination Rules

### Before Examination
1. Collect Hall Ticket from department
2. Check exam schedule on notice board
3. Report any discrepancy in registration
4. Clear any dues (fee, library, etc.)

### During Examination
1. Arrive 30 minutes before exam
2. Carry valid ID card and Hall Ticket
3. No electronic devices allowed
4. No communication between students
5. Use only provided answer booklets
6. Do not leave hall before 1 hour

### Prohibited Items
- Mobile phones
- Smartwatches
- Bluetooth devices
- Programmable calculators (unless permitted)
- Written notes or chits

### Malpractice Consequences
| Offense | Punishment |
|---------|-----------|
| Possession of written material | Cancellation of that exam |
| Copying | Cancellation of semester |
| Impersonation | Debarment from university |
| Mass copying | Strict action including rustication |

## Revaluation and Improvement

### Revaluation
- **Fee**: ₹500-750 per subject
- **Apply within**: 15 days of results
- **Process**: Expert committee re-evaluates paper
- **Outcome**: Marks may increase, decrease, or remain same

### Challenge Valuation
- Apply if unsatisfied with revaluation
- Higher fee than revaluation
- Final decision binding

### Improvement Exam
- For students who want to improve grades
- Can appear in subsequent semesters
- Best of the two attempts considered

### Supplementary Examination
- For students who fail in SEE
- Conducted before next semester exams
- Maximum 4-5 attempts allowed per subject

## Backlog and Detention Rules

### Backlog
- Subject not cleared = Backlog
- Must clear all backlogs to graduate
- Backlog exams conducted every semester

### Year Back / Detention
| Condition | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| > 4 backlog subjects | Year back |
| Disciplinary issues | Detention |
| Attendance < 50% | Year back |

### Maximum Duration
- B.E.: 8 years maximum
- B.Arch: 10 years maximum
- M.Tech: 4 years maximum

## Project and Internship

### Final Year Project (8th Semester)
- **Credits**: 12-16 credits
- **Duration**: Full semester
- **Evaluation**: Internal guide + External expert
- **Components**: Synopsis, Mid-term review, Final review, Viva

### Internship
- **Duration**: 4-6 weeks (summer after 6th semester)
- **Credits**: 2-4 credits
- **Requirement**: Industry internship mandatory
- **Evaluation**: Report + Presentation

## Academic Integrity

### Plagiarism Policy
- All submissions checked through plagiarism software
- Maximum allowed similarity: 20%
- Consequences: Zero marks to expulsion

### Code of Conduct
- Original work only
- Proper citations required
- Group work contributions must be clear
- No sharing answers during exams
