## Academic Rules and Regulations

### Attendance Requirements

- **Minimum Attendance:** 85% attendance is required for eligibility to appear in Semester End Examinations (SEE)
- Students falling short of attendance may be detained from examinations
- Medical and other valid leaves may be considered on case-by-case basis

### Evaluation System

The evaluation is based on:
1. **Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE)**: Internal assessments, assignments, quizzes conducted throughout the semester
2. **Semester End Examination (SEE)**: Final examination at the end of each semester

### Grading System

SIT follows a standard grading system with the following features:
- Letter grades (A, B, C, etc.) with corresponding grade points
- CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) calculation
- Promotion criteria based on passing required number of courses

### Discipline Policy

- Strict policies against drugs and alcohol
- Emphasis on professional ethics
- Ragging is strictly prohibited
- Dress code and behavioral guidelines must be followed

## Admissions

### Entrance Examinations:
- **KCET (Karnataka CET)**: College Code - E16
- **COMEDK**: College Code - E125
- Management quota and NRI seats also available

### Eligibility:
- 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics
- Minimum marks as per VTU/AICTE guidelines

## Scholarships

### SIT Scholarship Awards:

**For B.E. Students:**
- CET Rank up to 1000: Scholarship equal to tuition fee for first year
- Subsequent years: Maintain CGPA of 8.5 or higher to continue scholarship

**For B.Arch Students:**
- CET Rank up to 25: Scholarship equal to tuition fee for first year
- Subsequent years: Maintain CGPA of 8.5 or higher to continue scholarship

### Other Scholarships:
- Government scholarships for SC/ST/OBC students
- Merit scholarships
- Sports scholarships

## Examination Process

### Internal Assessment:
- Minimum 2 Internal Assessment Tests per semester
- Assignments and quizzes
- Laboratory records and viva

### Semester End Examination (SEE):
- Conducted at the end of each semester
- Duration: 3 hours for theory papers
- Practical/Laboratory examinations
- Project presentations and viva for final year

### Results and Revaluation:
- Results announced within stipulated time
- Revaluation facility available
- Supplementary examinations for failed subjects
