This document provides direct, concise answers to the most frequently asked questions about Siddaganga Institute of Technology (SIT).

## Attendance Requirements

### What is the minimum attendance requirement at SIT?
**Answer**: The minimum attendance requirement is **85%** in each subject to be eligible for semester-end examinations (SEE).

### What happens if I have less than 85% attendance?
- Students with **75-84% attendance** may apply for condonation on valid medical/genuine grounds
- Students below **75% attendance** may be detained from examinations
- Medical leaves and other valid leaves are considered on a case-by-case basis

### How is attendance recorded?
- Attendance is taken at the start of each class period
- Late entry beyond 15 minutes is marked as absent
- Bunking classes is a serious disciplinary offense

## Admission Process

### How to get admission to SIT for B.E.?
There are 3 main routes for admission:

1. **Karnataka CET (KCET)**
   - College Code: **E16**
   - Process: KCET exam → KEA counseling → seat selection
   - Timeline: KCET in April-May, results in June, counseling in July-August

2. **COMEDK UGET**
   - College Code: **E125**
   - Process: COMEDK exam → COMEDK counseling
   - Timeline: Exam in May, counseling in June-July

3. **Management Quota**
   - 15% of seats available
   - Direct application to college
   - Selection based on 12th marks and entrance test

### What are the eligibility criteria?
- Pass 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics
- Minimum 45% marks in PCM (40% for reserved categories)
- Valid CET/COMEDK/JEE Main score

### Documents required for admission?
- 10th and 12th mark sheets
- CET/COMEDK score card
- Transfer Certificate
- Migration Certificate (if applicable)
- Caste/Category Certificate (if applicable)
- Aadhar Card
- Passport photos (10 copies)

## Grading System

### What grading system does SIT follow?
SIT follows the standard VTU grading system with:
- Letter grades (O, A+, A, B+, B, C, P, F)
- Grade points (10, 9, 8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 0)
- CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) calculation

### How is CGPA calculated?
**CGPA = Sum of (Credit × Grade Point) / Total Credits** across all semesters

### Grade point conversion:
| Grade | Points | Percentage Range |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| O (Outstanding) | 10 | 90-100% |
| A+ (Excellent) | 9 | 80-89% |
| A (Very Good) | 8 | 70-79% |
| B+ (Good) | 7 | 60-69% |
| B (Above Average) | 6 | 50-59% |
| C (Average) | 5 | 45-49% |
| P (Pass) | 4 | 40-44% |
| F (Fail) | 0 | Below 40% |

### Evaluation Components:
- **CIE (Continuous Internal Evaluation)**: 50 marks (Internal tests, assignments, quizzes)
- **SEE (Semester End Examination)**: 50 marks

## Exam Schedule

### When are the internal exams conducted?
**Internal Assessment (IA) Tests**:
- IA-1: 6-7 weeks after semester starts
- IA-2: 12-13 weeks after semester starts
- Duration: 1.5 hours each, 20 marks each
- Best 2 of 3 IAs are considered

**Semester End Examinations (SEE)**:
- Odd Semester: December (after winter vacation)
- Even Semester: May-June
- Duration: 3 hours for theory papers
