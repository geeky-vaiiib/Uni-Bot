# SIT Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## General Questions

### Q1: What is the full form of SIT?
**Answer**: SIT stands for **Siddaganga Institute of Technology**. It is located in Tumakuru (Tumkur), Karnataka, India. The institute was established in 1963 and is affiliated to Visvesvaraya Technological University (VTU), Belagavi.

### Q2: Is SIT a government or private college?
**Answer**: SIT is a **private aided college** run by the Siddaganga Education Society. It receives partial government funding and is affiliated to VTU. Being autonomous, it has the freedom to design its own curriculum and conduct examinations.

### Q3: What is the NAAC grade of SIT?
**Answer**: SIT has been accredited by NAAC with **'A' grade**. The college is also ISO 9001:2015 certified and has NBA accreditation for several programs.

### Q4: What makes SIT unique?
**Answer**: SIT is known for:
- Free food (Dasoha) for all students
- Strong placement record
- Autonomous status
- Historic connection to Siddaganga Math
- Affordable education
- Strong alumni network

## Admission Related

### Q5: What is the CET code for SIT?
**Answer**: The KCET code for SIT is **E16**. The COMEDK code is **E125**.

### Q6: What is the cut-off rank for CSE at SIT?
**Answer**: Cut-off varies each year. Typically:
- **CSE (General)**: Top 3000-5000 CET rank
- **CSE-AI/ML**: Top 4000-6000 CET rank
- **ISE**: Top 5000-8000 CET rank

These are approximate figures and change annually.

### Q7: Is there direct admission to SIT?
**Answer**: Yes, through:
- Management quota (15% seats)
- NRI quota
- Contact the admission office directly for management quota

### Q8: Can I get admission through JEE Main?
**Answer**: SIT primarily admits through KCET and COMEDK. JEE Main scores may be considered for management quota but are not the primary admission route.

## Academic Questions

### Q9: What is the minimum attendance required?
**Answer**: **85%** attendance is required in each subject to be eligible for semester-end examinations. Students with 75-84% may apply for condonation on valid grounds.

### Q10: How many backlogs are allowed?
**Answer**: Students can carry backlogs, but having more than **4 backlogs** may result in a year back. All backlogs must be cleared to receive the degree.

### Q11: Can I change my branch after admission?
**Answer**: Yes, branch change is possible after the first year based on:
- Availability of seats
- Merit (1st year CGPA)
- No backlogs
- Apply within specified deadline

### Q12: Is SIT an autonomous college?
**Answer**: Yes, SIT has **autonomous status** granted by UGC. This means:
- Own curriculum design
- Own examination system
- Degree awarded by VTU with autonomous status mentioned

## Hostel and Campus Life

### Q13: Is hostel compulsory for all students?
**Answer**: Hostel is not compulsory. Day scholars from Tumakuru and nearby areas can commute. However, hostel is recommended for outstation students.

### Q14: What is Dasoha at SIT?
**Answer**: Dasoha is the **free food program** at SIT, inspired by the Siddaganga Math tradition. All students (hostel and day scholars) can avail free vegetarian meals - breakfast, lunch, snacks, and dinner.

### Q15: Are laptops allowed in hostel?
**Answer**: Yes, personal laptops are allowed in hostels. WiFi connectivity is available in hostels.

### Q16: What is the hostel in-time?
**Answer**: 
- Boys: 9:00 PM
- Girls: 8:00 PM

Extended permissions may be granted for valid reasons.

## Placements

### Q17: What is the average placement package at SIT?
**Answer**: The average placement package is approximately **₹5-7 LPA** (varies by branch). CSE/ISE branches typically have higher averages.

### Q18: What is the highest package offered at SIT?
**Answer**: The highest package in recent years has been around **₹40-45 LPA** from companies like Microsoft, Google, and Amazon.

### Q19: Which companies visit SIT for placements?
**Answer**: Major recruiters include Microsoft, Amazon, Infosys, TCS, Wipro, Cognizant, IBM, Oracle, L&T, Bosch, and many more. 100+ companies visit the campus annually.

### Q20: Is placement guaranteed at SIT?
**Answer**: While SIT has a strong placement record (85-95%), placement is not guaranteed. It depends on student skills, academics, and market conditions.

## Examination

### Q27: When are the internal exams conducted?
**Answer**: Two internal tests are conducted each semester, typically in:
- 1st internal: 6-7 weeks after semester start
- 2nd internal: 12-13 weeks after semester start

### Q28: How is CGPA calculated?
**Answer**: CGPA = Sum of (Credit × Grade Point) / Total Credits across all semesters.

### Q29: Can I improve my grades?
**Answer**: Yes, you can appear for improvement exams in subsequent semesters. The better grade is considered.

### Q30: What happens if I fail in a subject?
**Answer**: You can appear for the supplementary examination. If you fail again, you can attempt the subject when it's offered next (typically same semester next year).

## Miscellaneous

### Q31: Is ragging a problem at SIT?
**Answer**: No, SIT has **zero tolerance for ragging**. Strict anti-ragging measures are in place. Any ragging incident leads to immediate disciplinary action including rustication.

### Q32: Are there any coding clubs at SIT?
**Answer**: Yes, there are several technical clubs including Coding Club, IEEE Student Branch, Google Developer Student Club, and more.

### Q33: Can I bring a two-wheeler to campus?
**Answer**: Yes, two-wheelers are allowed. Parking facilities are available.

### Q34: Is there a dress code at SIT?
**Answer**: Formal dress code:
- Boys: Formal shirts, trousers
- Girls: Salwar kameez, formal wear
- ID cards must be worn at all times
- Friday may have relaxed dress code

### Q35: How far is SIT from Bangalore?
**Answer**: SIT is approximately **70 km from Bangalore** city center. Travel time is about 1.5-2 hours by road. Tumkur is well connected by bus and train.
